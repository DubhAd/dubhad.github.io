This is a short summary of the generations of Zigbee adapter chips, mostly for my own handy reference, but given that the information is scattered around, may also be useful for others.

My sources for this are:

  • This GitHub issue in the Z2M Herdsman repo
  • This page on the Electromah site

TI chips

These are well supported by Zigbee2MQTT, and also supported by ZHA (with the right firmware).

Each chip will have different models with slightly different features. For instance one may have a built in amplifier, or may be more sensitive in one band than another (where they support multiple bands).

Current generation

If you're buying a new stick/board for use with Zigbee2MQTT, these chips are what you're looking for.
  • CC2652* - 2.4 GHz only chip, like most Zigbee chips
  • CC1352* - this is broadly similar to the CC2652, but also supports the ISM (sub GHz) bands
There's a range of options here, and more coming to market all the time.

Previous generation

Not a terrible choice, but no longer a good choice. Should still be fine for smaller setups
  • CC2538

Old generation

If you're looking for things to flash as a router, these will cope fine with that. Just don't buy them to use as coordinators.
  • CC2530
  • CC2531

EZSP

I've skipped the various DIY things, the devices you can modify or hack, focussing on things you can buy and either use directly, or easily reflash.
